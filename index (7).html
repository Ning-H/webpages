Daily Schedule
📵 Phone rule: No phone for the first 30 minutes after waking. If you use it before then, your whole morning focus window is compromised — the algorithm will eat the study session. Pill + coffee first, always.
7:00–7:15
Wake Up — Pill + Brew Coffee no phone
Get up, take pill, start the coffee. No phone at all. Just let your body and brain boot quietly.
7:15–7:30
Phone-Free Window Continues still no phone
Drink your coffee. Sit, stretch, look out a window. Your focus quality for the entire day gets set in these 30 minutes. Don't waste it on a screen.
7:30–8:00
🍳 Breakfast eat before studying
Non-negotiable — you know you can't focus hungry. Eat a real meal. Don't rush it. This is the fuel for your 8am reading block.
8:00–9:00
📖 Morning Book Reading no video courses
Reading only — no video courses, no labs, no quizzes in the morning. Your brain absorbs well but can't actively produce yet. Read whichever book is active this week: DDIA, Chip Huyen, System Design, or Robey. One hour, slow and focused.
9:00–9:30
🔵 Work Start — Plan Your Day 3 priorities only
Open Slack and email — scan, don't spiral. Before touching anything else, write your 3 work priorities for today. This 30 min sets the direction so you don't spend the day in reactive mode.
9:30–12:00
🔵 Morning Work — Meetings + Lighter Tasks
Meetings typically land here. Between meetings: replies, reviews, standups, async tasks. Don't burn deep focus on admin. Save the heavy thinking for afternoon.
12:00–12:45
🍱 Lunch + Real Break
Fully step away. Eat properly. A 10-min walk here dramatically improves your afternoon output. Don't eat at your desk.
12:45–4:00
🔵 Deep Work — Hardest Tasks close Slack
Your best focus window. Close Slack notifications. Do your most cognitively demanding work here — analysis, writing, strategy, complex problem-solving. Protect this block.
4:00–4:30
Work Wrap + Admin
Slack sweep, reply emails, write tomorrow's 3 work priorities. Close the work day cleanly. Hard stop at 4:30.
4:30–6:00
🐕 Dog Park — Sacred Reset
Non-negotiable. Movement + fresh air + your dog = the reason your 7pm cert session actually works.
6:00–7:00
Dinner + Decompress Tue · Thu: gym here
Eat well and decompress. Tue/Thu: gym 6–6:45pm after dog park, then eat quickly before 7pm cert.
7:00–9:30
🟣 Cert Deep Work — Peak Window your prime time
Videos, labs, hands-on practice. This is when your brain is actually ready for active learning. No reading — this is doing time. Rotate certs by phase (see rotation below).
9:30–10:00
📝 Cert Review + Notes
Write 3 things you learned tonight. Close the loop. Don't skip — this is how it sticks.
10:00–10:30
Life Admin (optional)
Reimbursements, resume, personal emails. Only if needed. Hard stop at 10:30.
10:30–11:00
Wind Down — No Screens
No phone. Write tomorrow's 3 work priorities if you haven't yet. This is how you stop the anxiety loop before sleep.
Work Productivity System — Finish Strong by 4:30
9:00am
Write 3 priorities before touching anything
Not a to-do list — exactly 3 things that would make today a win. Put them somewhere visible. This is the single best thing you can do to not lose the day to Slack drift.
Meetings
Enter with one clear question to answer
Before each meeting, write one sentence: "I need X out of this." Walk out with that answer. Stops passive sitting and 45-min time losses.
12:45pm
Deep work: close Slack, phone face-down
Set a 90-min timer. No Slack, no email. Most things can wait. This afternoon block is where your real work output actually happens.
Rule
Stuck on something? Timebox it — 20 min max
Set a 20-min timer. If you're not unstuck, write where you left off and move on. Spiraling on one thing tanks the whole day and bleeds into your evening.
4:00pm
Shutdown ritual — 3 lines and close
Write: what you finished, what's still open, one thing for tomorrow. Close your laptop. This prevents the guilt-spiral of "I should still be working" that kills your cert session.
